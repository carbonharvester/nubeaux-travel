South Africa

Cape Town

Where mountain meets ocean. One of the world's most beautiful cities, surrounded by wine country and pristine beaches.

Why Cape Town

Cape Town consistently ranks among the world's most beautiful cities, and it's easy to see why. Table Mountain provides a dramatic backdrop to a city that combines natural splendour with cosmopolitan energy. The Winelands are just 45 minutes away. The beaches of Camps Bay rival any in the Mediterranean. And the food scene has evolved into one of Africa's most exciting.

For travellers, Cape Town offers rare versatility. You can hike Lion's Head at sunrise, taste wine in Stellenbosch by lunch, and watch the sunset from a beachfront restaurant. The city works as a standalone destination, a gateway to South Africa's garden route, or the perfect bookend to a safari elsewhere in Southern Africa.

Our Favorite Experiences

  • Table Mountain — Cable car or hike to the summit
  • Winelands — Stellenbosch, Franschhoek, and beyond
  • Camps Bay — Sunset drinks on the beachfront
  • Cape Point — Dramatic scenery at the peninsula's tip
  • Bo-Kaap — Colorful historic neighborhood
  • Constantia — Historic wine estates in the city

When to Visit

Cape Town's summer (November-March) offers the best weather with warm, dry days. Shoulder seasons (April-May, September-October) are also lovely with fewer crowds. Winter (June-August) brings rain but also whale watching season and green landscapes.

At a Glance

Best for: Wine, beach, hiking, food

Currency: South African Rand (ZAR)

Language: English, Afrikaans, Xhosa

Visa: Not required (most nationalities)

Best Time to Visit

Peak: November - March

Shoulder: April - May, Sept - Oct

Whale season: July - November

Getting There

Cape Town International Airport (CPT) with direct flights from Europe, Middle East, and Johannesburg.

Trip Length

City focus: 3-4 nights

With Winelands: 5-7 nights
