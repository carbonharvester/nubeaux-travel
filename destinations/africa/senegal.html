Senegal

Senegal is West Africa's cultural heartbeat, where French colonial architecture meets vibrant Wolof traditions. Dakar's creative energy, the historic island of Goree, and the country's renowned music scene draw travellers seeking authentic African urban experiences.

Why travel to Senegal

Senegal offers a different side of Africa from the safari-focused destinations of the east and south. Travellers come for Dakar's thriving art galleries, live music venues, and food scene influenced by both French and local cuisines. The country is also one of the most accessible entry points to West Africa, with direct flights from Europe and a stable, welcoming tourism infrastructure. Goree Island provides an important historical perspective on the Atlantic slave trade.

How people experience Senegal

Most Senegal trips are centred on Dakar, with travellers spending several nights exploring the city's neighbourhoods, markets, and cultural sites. Day trips to Goree Island and the pink waters of Lac Rose are common additions. Some itineraries extend south to the Sine-Saloum Delta for birdwatching and traditional fishing villages, or north to Saint-Louis, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Senegal works well as a standalone cultural destination or combined with The Gambia.

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Senegal?

The dry season from November to May offers the most comfortable weather. December to February is peak season with pleasant temperatures. The rainy season from June to October can make travel more challenging but brings lush green landscapes.

Is Senegal safe for travellers?

Senegal is considered one of West Africa's safest and most stable countries for tourism. Standard precautions apply in urban areas, but the country has a long tradition of hospitality toward visitors.
