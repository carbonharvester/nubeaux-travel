Aruba

Where Caribbean meets desert. Pristine white-sand beaches, dramatic divi-divi trees, and year-round sunshine on the happy island.

Why Aruba

Aruba defies Caribbean expectations. Yes, there are the powder-white beaches and turquoise waters you'd expect, but there's also an unexpected desert landscape of cacti and wind-sculpted divi-divi trees. Located just off the coast of Venezuela, Aruba sits outside the hurricane belt, offering reliable sunshine and calm waters year-round.

The island delivers a sophisticated beach experience with world-class resorts, excellent dining, and a welcoming culture that blends Dutch heritage with Caribbean warmth. Whether you're seeking relaxation on Eagle Beach, adventure in Arikok National Park, or romantic sunsets in the California Lighthouse area, Aruba rewards with consistent beauty.

Nia's Favorite Experiences

  • Eagle Beach — Consistently ranked among the world's best beaches
  • Arikok National Park — Dramatic desert landscapes and natural pools
  • California Lighthouse — Stunning sunset views and desert scenery
  • Oranjestad — Colorful Dutch colonial architecture
  • Palm Beach — Vibrant strip of resorts and water sports

When to Visit

Aruba enjoys year-round sunshine with average temperatures around 82°F (28°C). The island sits outside the hurricane belt, making it a reliable destination anytime. High season runs December through April with slightly cooler temperatures and less rain. The shoulder seasons offer better rates with equally beautiful weather.

At a Glance

Best for: Beach, relaxation, couples

Currency: Aruban Florin (AWG), USD widely accepted

Language: Dutch, Papiamento, English

Visa: Not required (US/UK/EU)

Best Time to Visit

Peak: December - April

Value: May - August

Weather: Year-round sunshine

Getting There

Queen Beatrix International Airport (AUA) with direct flights from major US cities, Amsterdam, and other Caribbean islands.

Trip Length

Weekend: 3-4 nights

Full relaxation: 5-7 nights
