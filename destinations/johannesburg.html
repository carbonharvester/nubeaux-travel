South Africa

Johannesburg

Africa's city of gold. A creative capital where art, history, and culinary innovation collide. The perfect gateway to safari.

Why Johannesburg

Johannesburg is not the South Africa of postcards. It's grittier, more complex, and infinitely more interesting. This is the economic engine of the continent, a city that has reinvented itself from mining town to creative hub. The streets of Maboneng pulse with galleries, restaurants, and markets. Rosebank has become a destination for luxury shopping. And the hotels have elevated to meet global standards.

For travellers, Jozi (as locals call it) serves two purposes: it's a destination in its own right for those seeking urban Africa, and it's the ideal gateway to safari country. Many of our itineraries begin or end here, allowing time to decompress, explore, and experience the city before heading into the bush.

Experiences

  • Apartheid Museum — Essential for understanding South Africa's history
  • Maboneng District — Street art, studios, and Sunday markets
  • Constitution Hill — Historic prison turned cultural precinct
  • Keyes Art Mile — Rosebank's art gallery corridor
  • Neighbourgoods Market — Saturday food market in Braamfontein
  • Soweto — Guided tours of this historic township

When to Visit

Johannesburg is pleasant year-round due to its altitude (1,753m). October to April is warmer with afternoon thunderstorms. May to September is cooler and dry. The city never really has a "low season" as it's primarily a business destination.

At a Glance

Best for: Art, culture, gastronomy, safari gateway

Currency: South African Rand (ZAR)

Language: English, Zulu, Afrikaans

Visa: Not required for most nationalities (90 days)

Best Time to Visit

Year-round destination

Peak: September - November

Note: December/January is holiday season

Getting There

O.R. Tambo International Airport (JNB) is Africa's busiest hub. Direct flights from London, Dubai, New York, and across Africa.

Trip Length

Standalone: 2-3 nights

Safari add-on: 1-2 nights pre/post
