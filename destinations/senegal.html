West Africa

Senegal

Where African culture meets Atlantic waves. Vibrant Dakar, historic Goree Island, and the warmth of Teranga hospitality.

Why Senegal

Senegal is West Africa's gateway, a country that pulses with creativity, music, and warmth. Dakar is one of Africa's most dynamic cities, where contemporary art galleries sit alongside bustling markets, and the sound of mbalax music fills the streets. The concept of "Teranga," or hospitality, isn't just a word here, it's a way of life.

Beyond the capital, Senegal offers historic weight at Goree Island, a UNESCO World Heritage site that stands as a powerful reminder of the transatlantic slave trade. The pink waters of Lac Rose, the wildlife of Djoudj National Bird Sanctuary, and the beaches of the Petite Cote round out a destination that rewards curious travellers.

Experiences

  • Goree Island — Historic island with powerful significance
  • Dakar Art Scene — Galleries, street art, and the IFAN Museum
  • Lac Rose — The famous pink lake north of Dakar
  • Saint-Louis — Colonial architecture and jazz heritage
  • Saly Beach — Relaxed coastal vibes on the Petite Cote

When to Visit

The dry season from November to May offers the best weather with lower humidity and minimal rainfall. December to February is peak season with comfortable temperatures. The rainy season (June to October) brings lush green landscapes but humidity and afternoon showers.

At a Glance

Best for: Culture, art, history, beach

Currency: West African CFA Franc (XOF)

Language: French, Wolof

Visa: Visa-free for many nationalities

Best Time to Visit

Peak: November - February

Shoulder: March - May

Rainy: June - October

Getting There

Blaise Diagne International Airport (DSS) near Dakar with direct flights from Paris, New York, and other African capitals.

Trip Length

City focus: 3-4 nights

Full experience: 7-10 nights
