Namibia

Onguma The Fort

Moorish-inspired architecture meets African wilderness on Etosha's eastern border.

Reviewed by Nia The Light

Nia's Take

Onguma The Fort is unlike any other safari lodge in Namibia. Its dramatic Afro-Moroccan architecture, complete with a tower bar, raw stone walls, and antique windows and doors, creates an otherworldly atmosphere. The lodge overlooks Fischer's Pan, a productive waterhole that draws constant wildlife, particularly during the dry season when it offers what Onguma describes as "probably one of the best sunset views in the whole of Namibia."

The fort-style main complex spans split levels, with 13 beautifully appointed suites: 11 Bush Suites, one exclusive Sultan's Suite in the main tower, and a romantic Honeymoon Suite. All suites overlook Fischer's Pan with uninterrupted views, and the Bush Suites feature outdoor showers and wood-fired hot tubs for starlit soaks.

What Nia Loved

  • Fischer's Pan waterhole — Elephants, lions, and more parading day and night
  • Tower bar — Panoramic views from the dramatic Afro-Moroccan tower
  • Wood-fired hot tubs — Private soaks under the stars in each Bush Suite
  • Art Gallery & Boutique — Browse curated African art and crafts
  • Photography opportunities — Golden hour light against otherworldly architecture
  • Sultan's Suite — For the ultimate splurge, the entire tower level to yourself

The Experience

Game drives into Etosha National Park depart early morning and late afternoon, with experienced guides who know every waterhole and hiding spot. The lodge sits on the Onguma Nature Reserve, a private concession where four of the Big Five roam freely, meaning you can also enjoy night drives and walking safaris not permitted in the national park itself.

The main guest area features a comfortable lounge, two dining areas, and that spectacular tower bar. Meals match the drama of the setting, with cuisine blending international influences and local ingredients. Free Wi-Fi keeps you connected, while the swimming pool provides afternoon respite between game activities.
