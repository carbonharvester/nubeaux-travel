Namibia

Onguma The Fort

Moorish-inspired architecture meets African wilderness. A dramatic safari lodge with panoramic waterhole views on Etosha's eastern border.

Reviewed by Nia The Light

Our Take

Onguma The Fort is unlike any other safari lodge in Namibia. Its dramatic Moorish-inspired architecture, complete with towers and ornate details, creates an otherworldly atmosphere that somehow feels perfectly at home in the African bush. The lodge overlooks a productive waterhole that draws a constant parade of wildlife, particularly during the dry season.

The rooms are spacious and thoughtfully designed, each with private balconies or patios that offer uninterrupted views of the waterhole. Waking up to elephants drinking at dawn or watching lions approach at dusk becomes the rhythm of your stay. The pool area, built to mirror the lodge's unique architecture, provides a beautiful spot for afternoon relaxation between game drives.

What We Loved

  • Waterhole views — Constant wildlife activity day and night
  • Unique architecture — Moorish design creates unforgettable atmosphere
  • Location — Perfect base for Etosha exploration
  • Night drives — Excellent guided experiences
  • Photography opportunities — Incredible light and wildlife compositions

The Experience

Game drives into Etosha National Park depart early morning and late afternoon, with the lodge's guides bringing extensive knowledge of local wildlife patterns. The lodge is positioned on the Onguma Game Reserve, a private concession bordering Etosha, which means you can also enjoy night drives and walking safaris not permitted in the national park itself.

Dining at The Fort matches the drama of the setting. Meals are served in the main lodge area with its sweeping views, or outdoors under the stars. The cuisine blends international influences with local ingredients, and dietary requirements are handled with care.
