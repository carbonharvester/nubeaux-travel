Mozambique

Anantara Bazaruto Island Resort

Exclusive island luxury in Mozambique's pristine marine national park. Where turquoise waters meet endless sand.

Reviewed by Nia The Light

Our Take

Anantara Bazaruto is the kind of place that feels genuinely remote. Reaching it requires effort: a flight to Vilanculos followed by a helicopter transfer over some of the most spectacular coastline in Africa. But that remoteness is precisely the point. Once you arrive, the world falls away.

The resort occupies a stunning position on Bazaruto Island, the largest in an archipelago that forms Mozambique's premier marine national park. Villas step down the dunes toward beaches that feel private even when they're not. The Indian Ocean is the color you hope for but rarely find.

What We Loved

  • The beaches — Pristine, uncrowded, impossibly beautiful
  • Marine life — Dolphins, dugongs, whale sharks in season
  • Sandbank picnics — Private lunches in the middle of the ocean
  • The villas — Spacious, well-designed, ocean views
  • Dhow sailing — Traditional boats, sunset cruises
  • Isolation — Genuine escape from the world

The Experience

Days at Anantara can be as active or as idle as you wish. The resort offers diving and snorkeling in the marine reserve, deep-sea fishing, dhow sailing, and island exploration. But the real luxury is doing nothing at all: reading on your deck, floating in the infinity pool, ordering room service and watching the ocean change color.

The dining is excellent, with fresh seafood the obvious highlight. The spa incorporates local ingredients and techniques. And the staff, largely Mozambican, bring genuine warmth to every interaction.

At a Glance

Location: Bazaruto Island, Mozambique

Style: Island beach resort

Rooms: 44 beach and pool villas

Best for: Honeymoons, romance, beach

Price Range

From: $800/night (half board)

Various meal plans available

Getting There

Fly to Vilanculos (VNX) via Johannesburg, then helicopter transfer to the island (15 minutes).

Best Time to Visit

Best weather: April - November

Whale sharks: October - March

Avoid: January - February (cyclone risk)
