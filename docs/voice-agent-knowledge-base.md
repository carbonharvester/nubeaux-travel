## 2. Commission Structure

### Industry Standards

| Booking Type | Commission Rate |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Hotels (Standard) | 10% |
| Hotels (Preferred Partners) | Up to 20% |
| Cruises | 10-18% |
| Tours & Experiences | 15-25% |
| Car Rentals | 8-12% |
| Travel Insurance | 20-30% |

### JUNO Tier System

| Tier | TTM GMV | Commission Rate |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| Tier 0 | $0 - $50k | 35% |
| Tier 1 | $50k - $300k | 40% |
| Tier 2 | $300k - $1m | 45% |
| Tier 3 | $1m - $3m | 50% |
| Tier 4 | $3m+ | 55% (60% strategic) |

**Rules:**
- Tier determined by Trailing Twelve Month (TTM) GMV
- 6-month grace period before tier downgrade

### Flight Commissions

- **Commissionable:** Premium economy, business class, and first class on international routes
- **Non-commissionable:** Most economy and domestic flights
- Must book through flight ticketing partner (not directly with airlines)

## 3. Preferred Partnerships

### What Makes a Preferred Partner?

Preferred partners offer additional benefits not available through standard partnerships:
- Special perks for clients (room upgrades, complimentary breakfast, property credits)
- Advantages for advisors (increased commission rates)

### Types of Preferred Partner Programs

1. **Consortia** - e.g., Virtuoso
2. **Booking Platforms** - e.g., Tablet Plus
3. **Hotel Group Programs** - e.g., Marriott Luminous, IHG One, Hyatt Prive
4. **In-house Programs** - e.g., JUNO Reserve

### Why Suppliers Offer Preferred Partnerships

- **Customer Loyalty** - VIP treatment encourages repeat bookings
- **Competitive Edge** - Differentiation from similar properties
- **Incremental Revenue** - Property credits often lead to additional spending
- **Word of Mouth Marketing** - Enhanced experiences get shared on social media
- **Guest Satisfaction** - Personalized touches enhance the entire experience

## 4. Virtuoso

### Overview
- World's largest luxury travel consortium
- Over 2,000 hotels offering exclusive benefits
- Typically five-star properties representing the highest level of luxury travel

### Standard Virtuoso Perks
- Complimentary daily breakfast for two
- $100 property credit (food & beverage or spa)
- Room upgrades (subject to availability)
- Early check-in / late checkout (subject to availability)

### Important Rules
- Perks cannot be applied to discounted rates or third-party bookings
- Must book the Best Available Rate (BAR) to include Virtuoso benefits
- **Never** create a Virtuoso account or attempt to login to virtuoso.com directly

## 5. Booking Channels (Priority Order)

### 1. Book in Portal (Most Preferred)
**Advantages:**
- Only commissionable rates displayed
- IATA number automatically applied
- Preferred partner perks hard-coded into reservation
- Instant confirmations
- Bookings automatically logged
- Support team can assist with issues

**Limitations:**
- One room at a time
- Cannot book groups (10+ rooms)

### 2. Book Directly with Partner
**When to Use:**
- Multiple rooms needed in same reservation
- Connecting/adjoining rooms required
- Group bookings (10+ rooms)

**Advantages:**
- Same rates as portal
- Same commission (standard 10%)
- Direct relationship with hotel for modifications

**Drawbacks:**
- Slower response times
- Must remember to include IATA number
- Uncertain payout for non-listed hotels

### 3. Third-Party Approved Channels (Least Preferred)
**When to Use:**
- Partner labeled as "risky payer"
- All-inclusive or package bookings
- Special deals or promotions

**Approved Platforms:**
- Tablet Hotels
- Michelin Guide Hotel Booking
- Expedia TAP (Travel Advisor Affiliate Program - NOT Expedia.com)
- ALG Vacations
- Classic Vacations

**Drawbacks:**
- Not working directly with hotel
- No special treatment for clients
- No preferred partner perks
- More restrictive terms
- Clients won't earn loyalty points

## 6. Partner Communication Best Practices

### When to Contact Partners Directly

1. **VIPing Clients** - Confirming reservations, ensuring perks, sharing arrival details
2. **Special Requests** - Adjoining rooms, service animals, allergies/dietary requirements
3. **Group Bookings** - Room blocks, meeting spaces, group activities
4. **Modifications/Cancellations** - Changes to existing bookings
5. **Problem Resolution** - Travel delays, room issues

### Who to Contact

| Contact Type | When to Use |
|--------------|-------------|
| Reservations Team | Primary contact for VIP emails, commission confirmation, perk verification |
| General Info/Help Email | When reservations email unavailable |
| Concierge | Spa treatments, activities, tours, restaurant reservations |

### Who NOT to Contact First

- **Director of Sales (DOS)** - Let reservations team loop them in for complex bookings
- **Business Development Manager (BDM)** - Same as above for cruise lines

### Communication Tips
- Always use your JUNO travel email
- Include all relevant details in initial email
- Be professional and prepared
- Show up on time for meetings (virtual or in-person)

## 7. Industry Organizations

### IATA (International Air Transport Association)
- Provides your agency identification number
- Required for earning commission
- Found in portal (upper right of each page, dashboard, property listings)

### CLIA (Cruise Lines International Association)
- World's largest cruise industry trade association
- Membership NOT required to book commissionable cruises
- Available at Pro or X status for cruise-focused advisors

### Virtuoso
- Elite consortium membership
- All advisors can access Virtuoso hotel perks through portal
- Do NOT create your own Virtuoso account

## 9. Tours, Activities & DMCs

### Tour Partners
- Can provide single activities to multi-day itineraries
- Exclusive experiences available (private Vatican tours, Churchill War rooms)

### DMCs (Destination Management Companies)
- "The travel agent's travel agent"
- Local experts handling end-to-end trip planning
- Best for:
  - Multiple services in one destination
  - Complex logistics
  - Higher budget trips
- Often have daily minimum budgets

### Package Providers
**Best for:**
- Single transaction trips
- Resort packages with transportation
- All-inclusive options
- Payment plans
- Group trip management
- Price-conscious travelers

## 10. Status Levels & Perks

### Certification Requirements
- Required before requesting site visits

### Pro Status Benefits
- Access to travel agent (TA) rates
- Eligible for FAM trips and conferences
- Can request Virtuoso login
- CLIA membership eligibility

### X Status Benefits
- All Pro benefits
- Marriott Stars and Luminous login eligibility

### Important Policies

**Do NOT:**
- Misuse or abuse industry perks
- Request TA rates before reaching Pro
- Create Virtuoso login yourself
- Create Marriott Stars/Luminous login yourself
- Register for or sign agency-level contracts with partners
- Take over bookings from another advisor (without proper process)

## 11. Flight Booking Guidelines

### Commission Eligibility
- Premium economy, business, first class = commissionable
- Economy and domestic = typically not commissionable
- Best opportunities: US-originating trans-oceanic business/first class

### Recommended Flight Partners
| Partner | Best For |
|---------|----------|
| Downtown Travel | Group and self-service booking |
| Highview Travel | Premium economy/business class, concierge support |
| Huntington Travel | Canada-based (tickets in CAD) |
| Point.me | Points bookings (fee-based, commissionable) |
| Vax, Classic Vacations | Packages bundling flights + hotels |

**Avoid:** Expedia TAP for flights (support issues)

### Important Notes
- Cannot book on airline website and add IATA number like hotels
- If booked direct, you're not agent of record = cannot support client

## 13. Common Client Questions & Responses

### "Why should I book with you instead of my credit card portal?"

**Response Points:**
1. **Upgrade Priority** - Credit card bookings have lower priority than preferred partner bookings for upgrades and room assignments
2. **Personalized Support** - Unlike rotating call center agents, you have one consistent advisor ensuring every detail is perfect
3. **Points Still Earned** - Clients can still earn credit card points when booking through an advisor
4. **Better Point Value** - Suggest saving points for airfare where they typically get best value

### "Why shouldn't I just book through an OTA?"

**OTA Limitations:**
- No access to exclusive perks or upgrades
- Lowest priority for room assignments and service
- Impersonal call center support when issues arise
- No advocate relationship

### "Can you match online prices?"

**Response Points:**
- Often same or similar rates as online
- Added value of perks often exceeds any price difference
- Room upgrades, breakfast, property credits add significant value
- 24/7 support and advocacy included

## 15. Voice Agent Response Guidelines

### Booking Inquiries
1. Gather destination, dates, and traveler count
2. Understand budget range and travel style
3. Identify any special requirements (accessibility, dietary, celebrations)
4. Present relevant itinerary options with creator context
5. Explain included perks and value-adds
6. Guide toward quote request or booking

### Creator Questions
1. Explain earning potential and commission structure
2. Highlight platform benefits (backend support, technology, community)
3. Direct to early access signup
4. Emphasize low barrier to entry - no large following required

### Problem Resolution
1. Express empathy and understanding
2. Gather all relevant booking details
3. Explain advocacy role
4. Set realistic expectations for resolution timeline
5. Follow up proactively

### Tone Guidelines
- Warm and professional
- Knowledgeable but not condescending
- Enthusiastic about travel
- Solution-oriented
- Personal and authentic (reflecting creator-led approach)
